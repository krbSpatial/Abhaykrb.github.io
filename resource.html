Heritage Mapping with UAV

This was my undergrduate thesis project done back in 2022. I guess it was the fist ever thesis project that used UAV for heritage documentation at that time. DJI Phantom 4 Pro drone was used to capture 610 aerial images of the Bindhyabasini Temple, Pokhara, Nepal. The images were processed using Pix4Dmapper software to create a 3D model, orthomosaic, and topographic map of the temple site. The final products are intended to assist the temple management committee with preservation and maintenance, as well as to help visitors and promote tourism.

Publication: View Report

Landfill Site Suitability

This study employs AHP and Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis to identify optimal landfill sites in Butwal, a rapidly growing city in Nepal. The research integrates various spatial data layers, including drainage density, groundwater depth, land use, soil type, road networks, surface elevation, surface slope, and proximity to residential and protected areas. By applying MCDA techniques, the study evaluates and ranks potential sites based on environmental, social, and technical criteria, aiming to minimize adverse impacts and ensure sustainable waste management solutions.

Publication: Bhusal KR, Banstola P, Adhikari S (2023). Landfill site selection using GIS and Multicriteria Decision Analysis : A Case Study of Butwal Sub-Metropolitan City. J Adv Res Geo Sci Rem Sens 2023; 10(1&2): 20-28

Urban Island Heat Effect

This research assess the rapid change in LULC in Rupandehi district, one of the rapidly urbanizing district of Nepal and access the relation between LULC, LST, NDVI and NDBI for year 2003, 2013 & 2023. The paper has been accepted for publication at Open Access Research Journal and will be available on mid of September. The findings of this study suggested the need for green infrastructure and strategic land use planning to combat rising land surface temperatures and maintain eco-logical balance.
